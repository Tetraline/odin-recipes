There are three main display parameters available to give to css boxes:

  • block
  • inline
  • inline-block

These are slightly confusing so let's give an example of each one.

To demonstrate, let's put the words 'cheese' and 'sauce' into their own boxes in the following sentence. Boxes will be outlined with blue outlines.

'Two ingredients in pizza are cheese and sauce. Pizza is of Italian origin.'

Our two ingredient words will be outlined with blue, while the broader sentence will be outlined in black. Onto every ingredient element we will try to apply some padding, with the CSS:

      
        div.ingredient {
            padding:2em;
        }

display: block

This is the default way that boxes arrange themselves, from top to bottom with a new line for each box. We can see that the request for padding around the words 'cheese' and 'sauce' were granted.

Two ingredients in pizza are
cheese
and
sauce
. Pizza is of Italian origin.

display: inline

The inline setting allows a box, or multiple boxes, to be included in one coherent line. The requested horizontal padding was granted, but the vertical padding was not respected.

Two ingredients in pizza are
cheese
and
sauce
. Pizza is of Italian origin.

display: inline-block

The inline-block setting is a hybrid, the two ingredients appear on the same line as the rest of the sentence and the requested padding is respected in all directions.

Two ingredients in pizza are
cheese
and
sauce
. Pizza is of Italian origin.
